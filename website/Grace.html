These satellites are on a mission to understand our Earth better. They do this by looking at something we can't see called "gravity." Gravity is what makes things fall to the ground, like when you drop a ball. But it also affects our Earth in many other ways.

These satellites have been doing their job for a long time - 15 years! During this time, they've been like the Earth's detectives, always keeping an eye on what's happening down here.

Water Secrets:

One of the coolest things they do is watch how water moves around the Earth. They can see when there's too much water, like during floods, or when there's not enough water, like during droughts. This helps us prepare for and understand these natural events.

Ice and Sea Levels:

They also watch the ice at the North and South Poles. When the ice melts, it makes the sea levels rise. These satellites help us figure out how much the sea levels are rising, which is important because it can affect places where people live.
